Privacy policy

How BANUB LTD collects, uses and protects personal information.

Last updated: 18 June 2026

BANUB LTD ("BANUB", "we", "us" or "our") is a company registered in England and Wales under company number 16733217. Our registered office is 35 Sun Street, Lancaster, England, LA1 1EW. For the purposes of UK data protection law, BANUB LTD is the controller of personal information collected through this website and in connection with our customer service and order-request activities.

This policy explains what information we collect, why we use it, who we may share it with, how long we keep it and the rights available to you.

1. Information we collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information:

  • Identity and contact information: name, email address, telephone number and correspondence details.
  • Delivery information: billing or delivery address, postcode, country and delivery instructions.
  • Order information: products selected, quantities, order reference, order status, returns, refunds and customer-service history.
  • Payment information: where payment services are introduced, payment card details will normally be collected and processed directly by an authorised payment provider. We may receive limited payment information such as payment status, transaction reference and the last digits of a payment card.
  • Account and preference information: account email, newsletter preference, cookie choice and saved browser preferences.
  • Technical and usage information: IP address, browser type, device information, operating system, pages viewed, timestamps, referring page and information stored through cookies or local browser storage.
  • Enquiry information: information included in contact forms, emails, complaints, return requests or other communications.

2. How we collect information

We collect information directly from you when you browse the website, add products to your cart, submit an order request, contact us, create a local account, subscribe to updates or request a return. We may also receive limited information from service providers used for hosting, delivery, payment processing, fraud prevention, analytics, communications and technical support.

3. How and why we use your information

We may use personal information to:

  • respond to enquiries and take steps requested by you before entering into a contract;
  • review and confirm order requests, product availability, delivery arrangements and payment instructions;
  • process and fulfil confirmed orders;
  • provide customer service and administer cancellations, returns, refunds and complaints;
  • maintain records for accounting, tax, fraud-prevention and legal purposes;
  • protect the security and integrity of the website and our business;
  • improve our products, website and customer experience;
  • send service communications relating to an order or enquiry;
  • send marketing communications where you have consented or where otherwise permitted by law.

4. Lawful bases

Under UK data protection law, we rely on one or more of the following lawful bases:

  • Contract: processing is necessary to take steps at your request before entering into a contract or to perform a contract with you.
  • Legal obligation: processing is necessary to comply with tax, accounting, consumer-protection or other legal duties.
  • Legitimate interests: processing is necessary for our legitimate interests in operating, securing and improving the business, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Consent: where you have given a clear choice, for example for certain marketing or non-essential cookies. You may withdraw consent at any time.

5. Marketing communications

We will only send marketing emails where permitted by law. You can unsubscribe at any time using the option provided in a message or by contacting us. Unsubscribing from marketing will not stop essential service messages about an order, return or enquiry.

6. Cookies and browser storage

The website uses essential browser storage to operate features such as the shopping cart, order requests, cookie preferences and local account details. Optional analytics or advertising technologies should only be used after the appropriate notice and consent are provided. More information is available in our Cookie policy.

7. Sharing your information

We may share relevant personal information with carefully selected third parties where necessary, including:

  • website hosting, cloud storage, email and IT service providers;
  • payment processors and banking service providers;
  • couriers, fulfilment partners and suppliers involved in delivering an order;
  • fraud-prevention, identity-verification and security providers;
  • professional advisers, insurers, accountants and legal advisers;
  • public authorities, regulators, courts or law-enforcement bodies where required by law;
  • a buyer or successor in connection with a merger, sale, restructuring or transfer of the business.

Service providers are expected to use information only for the agreed purpose and to protect it appropriately.

8. International transfers

Some service providers may process information outside the United Kingdom. Where personal information is transferred internationally, we will use a lawful transfer mechanism where required, such as an adequacy regulation, the UK International Data Transfer Agreement, the UK Addendum to approved standard contractual clauses, or another recognised safeguard.

9. How long we keep information

We retain personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was collected. Retention periods depend on the type of information and may include:

  • order, payment and accounting records for the period required by tax and company law;
  • customer-service and complaint records for as long as needed to resolve an issue and defend legal claims;
  • marketing preferences until you unsubscribe or we determine the information is no longer current;
  • technical and security records for a limited period needed to investigate incidents and protect the website.

When information is no longer required, we will delete, anonymise or securely dispose of it.

10. Security

We use reasonable organisational and technical measures designed to protect personal information against accidental loss, unauthorised access, alteration or disclosure. No online transmission or storage system can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

11. Your rights

Subject to applicable law and any relevant exemptions, you may have the right to:

  • request access to your personal information;
  • request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information;
  • request deletion of information in certain circumstances;
  • request restriction of processing;
  • object to processing based on legitimate interests or to direct marketing;
  • request data portability where applicable;
  • withdraw consent where processing is based on consent;
  • complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office.

We may need to verify your identity before responding to a request. You can raise a privacy request through our contact page or by writing to our registered office.

12. Children

This website is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

13. Third-party links

The website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for their privacy practices, and you should read their privacy notices before providing personal information.

14. Complaints

Please contact us first so that we can try to resolve any concern. You also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner’s Office.

15. Contact us

For privacy questions or requests, use our contact form and state that your enquiry concerns privacy, or write to: BANUB LTD, 35 Sun Street, Lancaster, England, LA1 1EW.

16. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy to reflect changes in our services, suppliers or legal obligations. The latest version will be published on this page with an updated date.
